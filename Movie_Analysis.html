So as to facilitate the answer to some questions, i will create a function that willextract the min and the max simultaneously, by using the idxmin()/idxmax() function to get the row label of the minimum/maximum value

In[95]:
# Create a minmax function by using idxmin()/idxmax() function to get the row label of the minimum/maximum value.

def min_max(x):
    # calculate maximum value
    highest = df[x].idxmax()
    highest_details = pd.DataFrame(df.loc[highest])
    
    # calculate minimum value
    lowest = df[x].idxmin()
    lowest_details = pd.DataFrame(df.loc[lowest])  
    
    print('Movie which has highest '+x+' : ', df['original_title'][highest])
    print('Movie which has lowest '+x+' : ', df['original_title'][lowest])
    
    return pd.concat([highest_details, lowest_details], axis=1)

Conclusions

Throughout this data analysis, I found that:

'Star Wars' 'Avatar', and 'Titanic' are the most profitable movies, while the top movies based on revenue gained are 'Avatar', 'Star Wars' and Titanic'. Even though these films are top 3 in term of money earned, but according to popularity it's not the same thing and they are not even in the top 5. We can say that June and December are the months when movies have gained the highest profits. These findings show the effect of holidays and festivities on the movie profitability. In term of correlation, i found that there is a very high and positive correlation between profit and revenue. So, companies that have larger revenues could have higher profits. Also, there is a positive and significative correlation between revenue and budget. It means that there is a good possibility that movies with higher expenses result in better revenues. To dig even deeper, causality tests should be carried out. In the other hand, there is a weak statistical relationship between the profit and the movie rating and no relationship between the budget and the runtime. The top 5 dominant movie genres all over these years (1960 - 2015) are Drama, Comedy, Thriller, Action and Adventure. But this finding doesnt mean that they are the most profitable genres (the top 5 profitable are Animation, Adventure, Family, Fantasy, Science Fiction) and the most popular (Adventure, Science fiction, Fantasy, animation, action). When I dig a little deeper, I find that these genres according to profit and popularity varie quite a lot from year to year and have known a significant uptrend in recent years. Finally, searching for the top actors we found Robert De Niro, Bruce Willis and Samuel L. Jackson, the top directors: Steven Spielberg Clint Eastwood, Ridley Scott and the top production companies: Universal Pictures, Warner Bros and Paramount Pictures.

Limitations...

This is just an attempt to analyze a small sample of movie. The film industry has thousands and thousands more. Unfortunately, there were so much missing data, that I couldnt do this analysis to a very large sample. So these findings may differ if the sample were larger.
